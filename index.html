Mission

A personal mission to keep the internet useful, practical, and free where it matters.

My work sits at the intersection of development, systems, and public utility. I like building infrastructure, publishing resources, and turning technical knowledge into something that helps real users move faster, host better, learn more, and stay independent.

I care about technology because it can empower people, not just extract from them. That is the inner thread running through everything here: free services for internet users, practical development, open resources, responsible AI, and tools that reduce friction instead of adding more gates. If a project can make the web more accessible, more competent, or less annoying, I want to build it.

Free first, value first

People should get real utility before they ever hit limits, upsells, or unnecessary friction.

Practical over performative

I prefer infrastructure, scripts, audits, and resources that solve real problems over polished emptiness.

Teach while building

Publishing resources matters as much as shipping products because independence starts with understanding.
